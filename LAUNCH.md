## Substack post — "One link."

_Title candidates: "One link." / "A year of shipping, finally indexed." / "I stopped hiding my work."_

**Dek:** I have spent a year shipping and sending people to nothing. This is the fix.

---

I have a rule I broke for about a year.

The rule is: if you are building in public, build where people can see you. Don't scatter. Don't drop a GitHub repo here, a Vercel deployment there, a half-written Substack draft in a folder called `drafts-drafts-final-v2`. Put a stake in the ground. Make it easy to follow.

I was breaking the rule because I thought the work wasn't ready. "I'll build a site when Keep raises." "I'll write about the oracle when the whitepaper is done." "I'll open source the eval when it's been peer-reviewed by the five people who care." Every one of those is a reason to stay hidden, dressed up as patience.

So I sat down this morning and built the link.

**param-vaswani.vercel.app**

Keep is on it. Keep is the thing I actually care about — charity-forfeit commitment markets settled by your Whoop or your Oura. You set a goal. You stake money behind it. You miss the bet, the money goes to a charity you actively dislike. Beeminder's idea with real markets and a cryptographic oracle underneath.

The rest is the machinery. I built four MCP servers because I wanted Claude to talk to Whoop, to Spaceship, to Vercel, to its own hooks. I built five evals because the benchmarks I needed did not exist — can models actually pick the right tool from a menu of seventy-eight? Can they settle biometric markets without producing dangerous hallucinations? How many rewrites before a model destroys your voice? I wrote a paper about cross-model context handoff because I got tired of re-priming every time I switched between Opus and Gemini.

None of it matters if you cannot find it.

So now you can find it. One link. Everything.

If you are building the same way — obsessively, in too many directions, losing track of your own work — stop hiding it. It takes 90 minutes. I did it this morning with Claude Code and the voice in my head that I should have listened to twelve months ago.

Next post: the oracle is the moat. Why prediction markets on biometric data collapse if the settlement layer lies.

— Param
Bangalore · Apr 2026
